This document is the Refinement phase of the SPARC methodology (Specification,
Pseudocode, Architecture, Refinement, Completion). Refinement is the testing
story: how the design was verified, the checkers and adversarial batteries that
guard it, the gates it must pass, and the places where the design was tightened
during the build. The repository is FROZEN; this document describes how the built
code was verified and proposes no change.


PART ONE - THE VERIFICATION STRATEGY

The arm's whole value depends on one claim: that only the pack boundaries moved,
and everything else - the behaviour, the twenty-eight records, the dynamics, the
closure mechanism - was held constant. So the verification is built to PROVE that
claim rather than merely assert it, and to catch any drift.

Two proofs by byte-identity anchor the strategy.

• The narrated trace of the loop is proven byte-identical to the Wave 2 slice and
  to the atomic and loops arms, by a direct file comparison (diff). If a single
  hop, token, or narration line differed, the comparison would fail. It does not.

• The twenty-eight structure records are proven byte-identical to the slice, by
  regenerating them from the five stratum packs and comparing every
  content-addressed identifier. Because each identifier is a Secure Hash
  Algorithm 256-bit (SHA-256) digest of a canonical form of the record, an equal
  identifier is proof of equal content. Every identifier matched, so distributing
  the minting by stratum cost the data nothing.


PART TWO - THE FIVE GATES

Each gate is a script under bin/ that exits zero on success. Together they are the
acceptance criteria of the Specification made executable.

Gate one - the loop runner (bin/run_slice.sh, driving bin/run_slice.pl). It ticks
the reentrant loop for N laps and then proves closure, not by inspection but by
four exact conditions checked in code: the hop count is three times N plus one;
the token sequence is the strictly increasing list one through that number; the
acting-region sequence is cortex followed by N repetitions of striatum, thalamus,
cortex; and the cortex re-entered N plus one times. Only when all four hold, and
the loop reported completion within a thirty-second bound, does the verdict read
pass and the script exit zero. A stall (no completion within the bound) or any
failed condition yields fail and a non-zero exit.

Gate two - the layer checker (bin/check_layers.sh). It loads the platform's
unmodified layer construct by its real path and runs its directory-scoped report
and check over the arm's eight packs. It exits zero only when there is no upward
edge. This gate also carries the arm's positive result: the report shows the five
stratum packs at layers two through six tracking the Causalontology ordinals four,
six, seven, nine, and fourteen.

Gate three - the coupling checker (bin/check_no_coupling.sh). It proves the
closure discipline: zero region-to-region references. Because the three regions
are co-stratal and share one pack, this gate does intra-pack section analysis: it
splits the region_stratum module at its internal-region markers into the cortex,
striatum, and thalamus sections, strips comments so only live code is examined,
and confirms that no section calls another region's predicate family or even
names another region as a bare word. The structure-minting preamble, which
legitimately names all three regions as continuants, sits before the first
section marker and is correctly excluded.

Gate four - the structure validator (bin/validate_structure.sh, driving
bin/validate_structure.pl). It assembles the twenty-eight records from the five
stratum packs and runs three families of check: each record against its
JavaScript Object Notation (JSON) schema and the local semantic rules of
Causalontology 2.0.0; the cortisol relation classified as a skipping relation with
no missing-mechanism gap; and the Ed25519 provenance signature over that relation
verified. It writes each record to structure/<name>.json and exits zero only when
every check passes.

Gate five - the test runner (bin/run_tests.sh). It runs each pack's in-pack PLUnit
suite in its own SWI-Prolog process and exits zero only when all eight pass. Every
pack ships a suite, so no pack can enter the repository without a test and rot
unseen.


PART THREE - THE ADVERSARIAL BATTERIES

A check that always passes proves nothing. Two of the gates are deliberately
tested against a planted violation, so a green result is meaningful.

• The coupling checker is validated by injecting a cross-region call into one
  section and confirming the checker reports a failure. This shows the
  zero-reference result is earned, not vacuous. Its two independent rules (a
  direct call into another region's predicate family, and the bare appearance of
  another region's name in code) give defence in depth.

• The closure verdict is adversarial by construction: it does not trust that the
  loop ran, it recomputes the exact expected hop count, token sequence, region
  sequence, and cortex re-entry count and demands equality. A loop that closed by
  luck, in the wrong order, or one hop short would be refuted.

The per-pack PLUnit suites add targeted adversarial cases. The neurochemistry
suite checks that zero dopamine produces no weight change (the third factor truly
gates learning) and that elevated cortisol shrinks the same update. The
community_stratum suite checks that the cortisol relation, whose effect occurrent
is imported down from a different pack, still classifies as a clean ten-stratum
skip with no gap. The region_stratum suite checks that the heaviest pack exposes
all three region ticks and mints exactly fourteen schema-valid records.


PART FOUR - WHERE THE DESIGN WAS TIGHTENED

The build surfaced one structural surprise that refined the design, and it became
finding STRATA-1. The design expected the loop's three regions to sit at different
levels, which would have made the closure cross-pack, as in the atomic arm. The
slice's own data said otherwise: cortex, striatum, and thalamus are all brain
regions at ordinal nine. One pack per stratum therefore placed all three region
runtimes in one pack as internal sections, making the loop intra-pack, like the
loops arm.

The refinement this forced was to the coupling checker. A checker that compared
one pack against another would have had nothing to compare, and would have passed
vacuously. So the checker was rebuilt to split the single region_stratum module
into its three internal sections and analyse across the section boundary. The
zero-reference guarantee is thereby preserved even though the code cut did not
separate the regions - and the checker was proven non-vacuous by the injected
call described above.

A second refinement was a path consequence, noted honestly rather than hidden.
Because the region_stratum pack co-locates its structure records with its runtime,
loading it to run the ticks pulls in the Lattice, and loading it to validate its
records pulls in the grounding engine. So both the runner and the validator must
carry the platform's causal_core and lattice packs and the conformance harness on
their library path, even though the loop never mints a record. This is recorded as
a re-touch of earlier findings (P5 and ATOMIC-4) rather than treated as new.


PART FIVE - THE REGRESSION GUARD

Beyond the arm's own gates, a mini regression confirms the wider platform still
behaves. It is a ten percent spot-check run through the platform's harness: forty
of forty on the Abstraction and Reasoning Corpus for Artificial General
Intelligence version one (ARC-AGI-1), and twelve of twelve on version two
(ARC-AGI-2). These are the honest figures, 40/40 and 12/12; they are a spot-check,
not the full benchmark, and the full regression is deferred. A green mini
regression is a guard against gross breakage, not a claim of the full score.


PART SIX - REFLECTION ON THE REFINEMENT

The refinement philosophy here is that a granularity experiment earns trust only
if its invariants are proven, not asserted, and if its checks can fail. The
byte-identity proofs pin the held-constant claim; the four-condition closure
verdict and the injected-call coupling test make the two behavioural gates
adversarial; and the one design surprise (co-stratal regions) was handled by
tightening a checker rather than by bending the one decomposition rule. The result
is a measurement whose green gates mean what they say.
