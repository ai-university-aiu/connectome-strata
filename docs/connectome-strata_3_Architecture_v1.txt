CONNECTOME-STRATA ARCHITECTURE  (v1)

Repository: connectome-strata
Document: Architecture (file 3 of 6)
Version: 1

This document is the Architecture phase of the SPARC methodology (Specification,
Pseudocode, Architecture, Refinement, Completion). It describes the structure of
the repository: its packs and the strata they map to, the layer assignments, the
substrate, the coordination through the Lattice, the interfaces between packs,
and where the repository reuses the PrologAI platform read-only. The repository
is FROZEN; this document describes the built structure and proposes no change.


PART ONE - THE COMPONENTS

The repository has eight packs. A pack is a self-contained code module with a
manifest (pack.pl), a single Prolog module under prolog/, and a Prolog Unit test
(PLUnit) suite under test/. Three packs are substrate; five are stratum packs.

Substrate packs (not at any single stratum):

• neural_lattice - the coordination substrate. It realises stigmergy for state
  (leaving a mark in a shared medium) plus notification for reactivity (a region
  blocks on a queue and is woken by a write). It imports only the platform's
  Lattice library.

• causal_grounding - the shared Causalontology 3.0.0 minting vocabulary. It holds
  the fourteen helpers that stamp a content-addressed identifier, fold key-value
  fields, and derive the deterministic signing key. It is reused verbatim so that
  a record minted in any stratum pack has byte-identical content, and therefore a
  byte-identical identifier, to the same record in the slice.

• neurochemistry - the native dynamics. It holds the numeric laws of dopamine (a
  reward-prediction-error), cortisol (a slow suppressive tone), and the
  three-factor synaptic plasticity rule. By the grounding rule these are not
  Causalontology records, so they have no stratum and are kept as substrate.

Stratum packs (one per Causalontology stratum the slice touches):

• macromolecular_stratum - Causalontology ordinal 4, the molecular level. It owns
  the macromolecular stratum record, the glucocorticoid gene-expression
  occurrent, and one token episode. Three records.

• cellular_stratum - ordinal 6, the cellular level. The slice names this level but
  places no construct at it, so this pack owns only its own stratum record. One
  record. Its near-emptiness is a finding (uneven packs, STRATA-4).

• synaptic_stratum - ordinal 7, the synaptic level. It owns the synaptic stratum
  record, four synaptic occurrents (neurotransmitter release, dopamine release,
  corticostriatal drive, weight change), and the corticostriatal transform
  causal_relation_object (a purely synaptic cause-and-effect claim). Six records.

• region_stratum - ordinal 9, the brain-region level. It is the heaviest pack:
  fourteen structure records plus the whole runtime. It owns the stratum record,
  four continuants (cortex, striatum, thalamus, substantia nigra pars compacta),
  the region-level action-selection occurrent, the synaptic-plasticity
  realizable, four ports, two conduits, and the bridge; and it holds the three
  region ticks as internal sections.

• community_stratum - ordinal 14, the community-and-society level, the coarsest
  the slice touches. It owns the stratum record, the chronic-social-subordination
  occurrent, the layer-skipping cortisol causal_relation_object, and the
  Ed25519-signed provenance assertion over that relation. Four records.


PART TWO - THE LAYER ASSIGNMENTS

The platform's layer construct gives each pack a layer number and forbids any
import from a lower layer up to a higher one. This repository assigns layers so
that the five stratum packs track the Causalontology stratum ordinals. The rule
is: pack layer equals the rank of the stratum ordinal plus one.

    layer 0   neural_lattice          substrate (Lattice closure)
    layer 0   causal_grounding        substrate (minting vocabulary)
    layer 1   neurochemistry          un-stratified native dynamics
    layer 2   macromolecular_stratum  stratum ordinal 4
    layer 3   cellular_stratum        stratum ordinal 6
    layer 4   synaptic_stratum        stratum ordinal 7
    layer 5   region_stratum          stratum ordinal 9
    layer 6   community_stratum       stratum ordinal 14

Seven layers in total. Every real cross-pack edge runs from a coarser (higher)
stratum down to a finer (lower) one, which is a downward edge the layer rule
permits, so the ordinal order and the layer order agree and the layer order comes
essentially for free from the data layer. One honest caveat: nothing in the
platform binds a pack's declared layer to its stratum ordinal; the agreement is
maintained by hand and unchecked (finding STRATA-3), and an isolated stratum such
as cellular, imported by nobody, has a free layer choice the ordinal only fixes by
convention.


PART THREE - THE INTERFACES BETWEEN PACKS

There are nine intra-repository import edges across the eight packs (roughly one
and one tenth per pack), which sits between the loops arm (fewer) and the atomic
arm (more). The edges are:

• macromolecular_stratum imports causal_grounding.

• cellular_stratum imports causal_grounding.

• synaptic_stratum imports causal_grounding.

• region_stratum imports causal_grounding, synaptic_stratum, neural_lattice, and
  neurochemistry (four edges). It reaches DOWN to synaptic_stratum for the
  occurrent and transform identifiers that its ports, conduits, and bridge
  reference; it uses neural_lattice and neurochemistry for the runtime.

• community_stratum imports causal_grounding and macromolecular_stratum (two
  edges). The second is the cortisol SKIP: community (ordinal 14) reaches down to
  macromolecular (ordinal 4) for the gene-expression effect. Because the skip
  runs from a high ordinal to a low one, this extreme cross-stratal reference is
  the cleanest downward edge in the whole arm.

The coupling concentrates in the two packs that hold spanning constructs
(region_stratum and community_stratum); the leaf stratum packs each import only
the minting vocabulary.


PART FOUR - THE LATTICE COORDINATION

At runtime the three regions are three background threads spawned by the
platform's cyclic_actor. They never call one another. All coordination passes
through one shared Lattice nexus at a fixed address (a locus Uniform Resource
Identifier, or locus URI). The protocol:

• A region blocks on its own numbered phase cue with a Lattice await, using no
  processor time until a write wakes it (notification, not busy-polling).

• When a region acts, it takes its cue, does its work, and posts the next slot's
  cue carrying the whole state snapshot. Exactly one phase cue exists at any
  instant, so the ring is deterministic and only one region is ever active.

• The state snapshot (lap, token, weight, prediction, cortisol, and so on) is the
  stigmergic mark: state is shared by leaving it in the common medium, never by a
  direct message naming a recipient.

Because the three regions are co-stratal they live as internal sections of the
one region_stratum pack, so the reentrant loop is intra-pack. The coupling
checker therefore does intra-pack section analysis to confirm that no section's
code calls or names another region.


PART FIVE - THE PACK GRAPH (text diagram)

An arrow (->) points from importer to imported, and every edge is downward. Each
pack's layer number is shown in brackets. The tree is read by indentation: an
indented line is imported by the pack above it.

    community_stratum [6]
        -> causal_grounding [0]
        -> macromolecular_stratum [2]   (this edge is the cortisol SKIP)
               -> causal_grounding [0]

    region_stratum [5]
        -> causal_grounding [0]
        -> synaptic_stratum [4]
               -> causal_grounding [0]
        -> neural_lattice [0]
        -> neurochemistry [1]

    cellular_stratum [3]
        -> causal_grounding [0]

    substrate band (imports only the platform, no intra-repository edge):
        neural_lattice [0]    -> platform library(lattice)
        causal_grounding [0]  -> platform library(causal_core), library(signing)
        neurochemistry [1]    -> pure arithmetic leaf, no import

Runtime view of the region_stratum pack (one pack, three internal sections, all
at ordinal 9):

    region_stratum pack
        internal section cortex    is the origin and re-entry point
        internal section striatum  is the dopamine-gated plasticity site
        internal section thalamus  is the relay that closes the loop
        all three coordinate ONLY through the shared Lattice nexus, in the
        cue order phase_0 -> phase_1 -> phase_2 -> phase_0, with zero
        region-to-region calls


PART SIX - REUSE OF THE PROLOGAI PLATFORM (read-only)

The wider PrologAI platform is reused unmodified and read-only, resolved through
the PROLOGAI_HOME environment variable (default: the local checkout at
/home/ccaitwo/PrologAI). The bin/ scripts build a SWI-Prolog library path over
the arm's eight packs plus the following platform packs and the conformance
harness. The repository pins no platform commit hash of its own; it depends on
the local checkout referenced by PROLOGAI_HOME.

• library(lattice) - the shared coordination store with await and notify.

• library(actors) - the cyclic_actor background threads.

• library(causal_core) - the Causalontology 3.0.0 engine (content identity, kind
  inference, semantic rules, the classification and skip-gap algorithms).

• library(layer) - the strict layer-rule construct (its directory-scoped report
  and check predicates), run over the arm by bin/check_layers.sh.

• the causalontology_conformance harness under tests/ - the JSON-schema
  interpreter (schema_check), the pure-Prolog Ed25519 signing layer (signing,
  ed25519), and the eighteen schemas - used by the structure validator.

Every gap the arm found in these platform pieces is recorded in LEDGER.md, never
patched. This keeps the platform frozen and the measurement honest.


PART SEVEN - REFLECTION ON THE ARCHITECTURE

The architecture's defining decision is that the pack boundary is a data-layer
kind (the stratum), not a code-only idea. The benefit is a genuine alignment: the
layer order tracks the ordinals, structure minting localises by level, and the
extreme layer-skip is a clean downward edge. The accepted cost is that constructs
which span levels (ports, conduits, bridge, skip) have no single-stratum home and
pile into the coarser stratum pack, and that the runtime loop, being
single-stratum, lives entirely inside one heavy pack. The architecture makes both
the benefit and the cost visible and checkable, which is exactly what a
granularity experiment is for.
